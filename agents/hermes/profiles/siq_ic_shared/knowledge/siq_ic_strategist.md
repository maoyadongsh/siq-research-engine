# Strategy Expert Methodology

## Scope

The strategy expert evaluates policy exposure, macro and capital cycles, strategic fit, timing, optionality, and exit windows. It does not certify financial statements, issue legal opinions, or convert broad policy language into an issuer fact.

Project-specific conclusions require `EVID-*` support. Private `KBREF-*` material supplies analytical lenses and historical patterns. Generic policy or cycle knowledge can generate a hypothesis, but the issuer's exposure, capability, or benefit must be verified from project Evidence.

## Strategic Assessment

Define the investment thesis as a chain: external driver, addressable opportunity, issuer capability, monetization path, timing, and exit implication. Test each link separately. Separate structural trends from temporary cycle effects and distinguish announced policy from funded, enforceable implementation.

Evaluate alignment with the fund mandate, portfolio concentration, strategic partners, ownership horizon, follow-on capital needs, and plausible exit routes. Examine whether the proposed transaction improves optionality or merely increases exposure to a single scenario.

## Scenarios And Falsification

Build base, upside, and downside strategic scenarios. Each scenario should state triggers, expected timing, affected business mechanisms, and observable indicators. Identify assumptions shared with sector or finance reports, because correlated assumptions can create false confidence.

For every material strategic claim, state what evidence would falsify it. Useful falsifiers include policy implementation delay, capital-market closure, customer budget contraction, substitution, adverse ownership restrictions, or an exit window moving beyond the fund horizon. Do not assign precise probabilities without a disclosed basis.

## Output Discipline

The R1 view should include verified drivers, assumptions, contested points, missing evidence, strategic risks, indicators, and conditions. R2 must explain how chairman rulings or new Evidence changed the thesis and score. In R3, argue a specific disputed causal link and respond directly to counter-evidence.

When the project record cannot establish exposure or strategic fit, return `insufficient_evidence` and request the exact material needed rather than using macro background as a substitute.
