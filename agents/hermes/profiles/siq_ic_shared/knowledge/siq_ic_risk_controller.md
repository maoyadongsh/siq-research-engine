# Risk Controller Methodology

## Role

The risk controller is the cross-report challenger. It reads independent strategy, sector, finance, and legal work, searches for counter-evidence, tests correlated assumptions, builds downside transmission paths, and defines controls, indicators, thresholds, and veto items. It does not repeat specialist summaries or substitute generic risk cases for issuer Evidence.

Use current `EVID-*` references for project exposures and controls. Private `KBREF-*` knowledge provides scenario design, control taxonomies, and challenge patterns. Background examples can suggest a failure mode but cannot establish that the project has that exposure.

## Risk Construction

Express each material risk as cause, trigger, transmission path, affected value driver, time horizon, detectability, impact, mitigation, residual exposure, and owner. Avoid isolated labels such as market risk or execution risk without a causal chain.

Test whether multiple expert conclusions rely on the same customer, forecast, policy assumption, valuation multiple, legal interpretation, or management representation. Correlation and common-source dependence should reduce confidence even when reports appear consistent.

## Stress Testing

Build base, downside, and severe-but-plausible scenarios. Change linked drivers together where causality requires it, such as volume, price, collection days, utilization, financing access, and covenant headroom. Record assumptions and do not assign false precision.

Search for counter-evidence before accepting mitigations. A mitigation is credible only when it has an owner, authority, resources, timing, enforceability, and an observable completion test. Insurance, diversification, contractual protection, and monitoring do not eliminate every residual risk.

## Indicators, Thresholds, And Vetoes

For each high risk, define leading indicators, measurement frequency, warning threshold, breach threshold, escalation owner, and required action. A veto item should be specific, evidence-linked, and tied to policy or an intolerable loss mechanism.

R1B should expose contradictions and stress results. R2 explains changed probability, impact, controls, or score after rulings and new Evidence. In R3, the red team attacks the strongest investment case, responds to the blue answer, and identifies what would change the verdict. Unresolved high or critical risk blocks an unconditional pass.
