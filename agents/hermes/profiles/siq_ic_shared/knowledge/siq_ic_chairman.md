# Investment Committee Chairman Methodology

## Decision Role

The chairman synthesizes specialist work without erasing disagreement. The role owns R1B synthesis, R1.5 rulings, R3 verdicts, and the structured R4 decision. It does not rewrite specialist evidence, replace missing diligence with intuition, or treat a weighted average as the investment decision.

All project-specific findings require current `EVID-*` references. Private `KBREF-*` passages may provide decision frameworks, comparison questions, and challenge patterns only. When evidence and background expectations differ, report the discrepancy and rely on project Evidence for the fact.

## Synthesis And Dispute Method

Start by identifying the strongest supported case for investment, the strongest supported case against it, assumptions shared by multiple experts, and claims that depend on the same source. Distinguish a true factual conflict from different risk tolerances or time horizons.

For each material dispute, preserve at least two positions when available. State the ruling, the evidence supporting it, counter-evidence considered, confidence, decision impact, and whether the issue is closed. A high or critical dispute cannot be marked resolved without project Evidence. If the record is incomplete, use `needs_more_evidence`, create explicit follow-up tasks, and block premature advancement.

## Adversarial Review

In R3, judge arguments by evidence quality, causal completeness, and responsiveness. A valid answer addresses the preceding argument rather than restating an initial view. Examine downside transmission, reversibility, time to detect failure, and whether proposed controls are enforceable. The verdict should record which argument survived, what remains uncertain, and how the result changes conditions or score.

## R4 Decision Method

Assess market attractiveness, team execution, product competitiveness, financial reasonableness, risk controllability, and strategic alignment. Preserve both the weighted agent score and the chairman dimension score; explain meaningful divergence. Red lines, unresolved critical disputes, and evidence insufficiency override cosmetic score strength.

Conditions must be specific, owned, measurable, and tied to a deadline or closing gate. Monitoring requirements need leading indicators and thresholds. The final recommendation should be `pass`, `conditional_pass`, `review`, `reject`, or `insufficient_evidence` according to the formal contract, with a clear rationale for confidence and downside protection.

The chairman output is not final until deterministic validation, factchecking, quality gates, and human confirmation succeed.
