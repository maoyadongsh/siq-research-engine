# Legal Scanner Methodology

## Scope And Source Hierarchy

The legal scanner evaluates entity status, ownership, authority, licenses, material contracts, litigation, intellectual property, employment, data, regulatory exposure, related-party matters, and transaction conditions. It does not infer legal status from a generic regulation summary.

Project conclusions require `EVID-*` references to the relevant filing, register extract, contract, certificate, legal opinion, correspondence, or diligence record. Private `KBREF-*` materials provide issue-spotting frameworks and authority-search methods. A background rule identifies what to verify; it does not prove issuer compliance.

## Review Method

Confirm the legal entity, ownership chain, beneficial ownership, capital history, transfer restrictions, board and shareholder authority, and consistency across corporate records. Identify encumbrances, nominee arrangements, option pools, side letters, and unrecorded rights.

For licenses and regulation, map each material activity to the required authority, territory, validity period, renewal condition, and consequence of non-compliance. Distinguish a filed application, provisional permission, full license, and actual compliance in operation.

Review material contracts for term, economics, termination, change of control, exclusivity, assignment, liability, indemnity, audit, data, intellectual property, and dispute provisions. Assess whether key technology is owned, licensed, employee-created, contractor-created, pledged, challenged, or dependent on third-party components.

## Severity And Remediation

For each issue, state the fact, applicable authority or contract basis, severity, probability where supportable, financial or operational consequence, remediation, owner, deadline, and transaction treatment. Separate curable documentation gaps from structural illegality or unenforceable rights.

Closing conditions and covenants must be objectively testable. High-severity unresolved matters require escalation and may create a veto. R2 must show how new documents or rulings change severity and conditions. In R3, answer the exact legal challenge and cite the governing project document or authority.

If the record cannot support a legal conclusion, use `missing` or `contested`; do not downgrade the issue merely because no adverse document was supplied.
