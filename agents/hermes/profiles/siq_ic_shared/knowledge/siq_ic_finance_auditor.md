# Finance Auditor Methodology

## Scope And Evidence Discipline

The finance auditor evaluates historical financial reliability, revenue quality, statement consistency, forecasts, valuation, liquidity, and sensitivity. It does not invent missing periods, silently normalize units, or present sector benchmarks as issuer results.

Every issuer number requires a current `EVID-*` locator. Private `KBREF-*` material supplies audit procedures, valuation methods, and benchmark questions only. Preserve period, currency, unit, scope, formula, and calculator trace for every material numeric conclusion.

## Historical Review

Reconcile the income statement, balance sheet, and cash flow statement across periods. Check opening and closing balances, cash movement, retained earnings, working-capital movements, debt, taxes, and non-cash items. Record unresolved differences instead of forcing a plug.

Analyze revenue by product, customer, geography, contract type, and recognition pattern where available. Test growth against receivables, contract assets, cash collection, returns, discounts, channel inventory, related parties, and customer concentration. Separate recurring operating performance from subsidies, fair-value changes, asset sales, capitalization choices, and other non-recurring items.

## Forecast And Valuation

Build forecasts from operational drivers rather than applying unsupported growth rates. Link volume, price, mix, capacity, utilization, headcount, working capital, capital expenditure, financing, and tax assumptions. Recalculate base, downside, and severe downside cases.

Use valuation methods appropriate to maturity and cash-flow visibility. Show comparable selection, normalization, valuation date, net debt, dilution, discount rate, terminal assumptions, and scenario range. A single-point valuation without sensitivity is insufficient.

## Risk And Output

Identify liquidity runway, covenant risk, refinancing dependence, cash conversion, contingent liabilities, and funding requirements. Define leading indicators and thresholds for material financial risks.

The formal report must expose source lineage and calculation traces. R2 identifies changed inputs, recalculation results, and score deltas. If evidence capability blocks reliable financial facts, return `insufficient_evidence` and specify the statements, schedules, confirmations, or model inputs required.
