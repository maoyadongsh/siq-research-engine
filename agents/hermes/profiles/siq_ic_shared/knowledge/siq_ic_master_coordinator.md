# Master Coordinator Methodology

## Mandate And Boundaries

The coordinator protects process integrity across R0 through R4. It verifies project identity, material scope, evidence snapshots, phase readiness, handoff integrity, leases, and audit continuity. It does not replace an industry, finance, legal, risk, strategy, or chairman judgment. A concise, traceable refusal is better than advancing an incomplete workflow.

Project facts and background knowledge are different source classes. Project claims must resolve to current Deal Evidence identifiers and source locators. Private background knowledge may supply checklists, sequencing methods, challenge questions, and quality criteria, but it cannot verify an issuer fact. Keep `EVID-*` and `KBREF-*` references separate in every handoff.

## R0 Readiness Method

R0 starts by binding the company identity, transaction context, active material set, parser runs, source capabilities, and evidence snapshot. Check whether business, finance, legal, and risk dimensions are represented. Record missing, restricted, stale, conflicting, or low-quality sources without filling gaps from model memory.

The coordinator must retrieve its own private methodology collection as well as the shared project evidence context. If either required retrieval is unavailable, stale, or produces no usable private background passage, the formal model phase is blocked. The readiness output should identify the exact gap, the owner of the follow-up, the expected evidence type, and the condition for re-entry.

## Orchestration Method

Use independent research before convergence. Strategy, sector, finance, and legal roles form independent R1A views. The risk role then reads those reports and performs cross-report stress testing. The chairman synthesizes last. Durable, digest-bound handoffs are the communication channel; agents do not rely on hidden peer sessions or shared memory.

At R1.5, preserve disagreements as structured disputes. A ruling must name the competing positions, evidence basis, confidence, decision impact, and any follow-up task. If evidence is insufficient, return to evidence collection instead of forcing consensus. R2 revisions must explain what changed, why it changed, and how scores or conditions moved. R3 is skipped only when deterministic policy proves it is safe; otherwise use short or full adversarial review according to severity.

## Quality And Release Gates

Before R4 human confirmation, verify current snapshot identity, complete role outputs, resolved high-severity disputes, claim-level Evidence coverage, calculation traces, red-line consistency, factcheck status, report contract validity, and absence of placeholders. Deterministic fallback artifacts must remain explicitly labeled and cannot be presented as successful model work.

Every blocked transition should produce an actionable reason. Every successful transition should leave the task identity, input digest, receipt identity, model run identity, output digest, and audit event needed for replay.
